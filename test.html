I brokke down on my summer vacation and re-read this entire thing in a couple of days.
Still breaks my heart everytime... Third re-read.
I feel like every serious incident ends in the second to worst case scenario and it irks me... That when I first read this, it forced me to continue reading in hopes that everything would end in a good ending.
It was an emotional rollercoaster then and it doesn't get any less so on the third read, it still hits hard in congruence with the nostalgia of the feelings from reading it for the first time.
It was a great disappointment to see Rui and Natsuo to call off the wedding due to the crash. I feel like even if they got married, as long as they kept taking care of Hina, she would eventually be able to let go of her loving feelings for Natsuo with the arrival of Haruka, leading her to rekindle her relationship with Shu, get engaged and have her own family while remaining in close proximity to the rest of them.
It felt like a huge betrayal, after seeing Natsuo and Rui so happy after confirming eachothers feelings, getting back together, Rui getting pregnant and them deciding to get married only to break them apart...
The amount of time spent shipping through all the trials and tribulations, to be promised the first place finish only to be given the participation award was really frustrating.
Realizing a good drama does not end the way people WANTED it to end, makes it greater than if they just cut it short happy go lucky.
It evoked many bouts of disbelief, grief and frustration and anything that can do that is, in my worldview ... a work of art.
